Logistics are insane - From China to my doorstep for 2$

10 mar 2026

I ordered a drawing tablet a few days ago, and I found myself checking the tracking status of the package every morning, because I just cannot wait. While doing so, I went down the deep rabbithole of how international logistics work, and I'll never look at a delay in my package the same way again.

I always thought "shipping" meant literally loading a million goods into a huge cargo ship and transporting them across borders, so I was pretty confused to see my tracking service saying it was "ready to take-off". For a very little amount of money, they are somehow reserving a reasonable amount of space on a plane. I can't imagine the fact that it is currently sitting at an airport in China literally ready to be boarded onto a plane.

But when you look into it, it starts making more sense, instead of reserving a dedicated space for each little item, they can just place them in the gaps between the larger boxes. And let's say plane can carry an X amount of cargo, while a ship can carry 10X, but let's think the plane can do 20 journeys in the same amount of time the ship takes to do one. The plane clearly carried more cargo in the same period of time (plane 20X, ship 10X). I'm sure it's not as simple as that, considering the expenses of operatin a plane is way different from that of a ship. The thing is, most of this cargo is being transported on normal passenger airlines, and those passengers basically pay for the journey.

All of this brings up another question, there are millions of packages being transported worldwide, and they have to keep track of each of them. It would've been easier, though not really easy, to track them if they were all stacked nicely in their own assigned sections. But the fact they they shove as much different types of items in the same box, and still tracking each of them is just wild to think about.

Packing everything is one thing, but they also have to manage everything through customs and then seperate each item into their own destination. All of this work so I can have whatever I want directly at my doorstep, for something as small as $2.
