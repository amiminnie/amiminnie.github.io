Timeline of my art - Time to take it seriously

07 mar 2026

I've always had a thing for drawing, I'd always draw little doodles on my school notes for example, and people seemed to like them. From that time onwards, I've been working on improving my art, I'm not saying it's perfect now, but way better than how it started. I've compiled a timeline of what my art looked like each year, enjoy!

< 2021 - Flipbook era

If I had to guess where my art started becoming actual "art" instead of just doodles along with notes, I'd give credits to the youtuber Andymation for making me obsessed with flipbooks. I mean OBSESSED because I'd draw them everywhere. I remember just cutting up tons of A4 sheets so I can bind them together with nothing but staples and make my own flipbooks. They weren't so great at first, only I could even turn them... barely, but the fact that I had to draw tens of drawings just for a few second clip meant that I was learning patience from it, the fact that you can't hurry when it comes to art. Unfortunately, all flipbooks I've made seem to be lost to time...

2021 - Taste of digital art

Being so obsessed with flipbooks, I was blown away to find out that you could make them without the hassle of cutting up all the paper, then play them right on the phone. I had just discovered FlipaClip. It was all I needed at the time. But it also made me a little lazy, as you can tell fromt my first animation; "The misteryus bix"

Since I was not yet used to drawing on the touch screen, I ended up just using emoji as sprites, which is something present me would never do. You can also tell that it was indeed my first time using that program, from the terrible lasso job I seem to have done. (click the preview to watch the video)

Fortunately though, younger me would also agree, because the next animation, "Runner for the TV", is fully original content! Sure, it looks like a 11 year old made it, because it was.

This is another animation I made during that time, while it has the same (not so great) art style, I have managed to add color to it. You can tell that I was still just drawing whatever, instead of planning the story ahead, I just went along with whatever I felt like i guess.

2022 - Figuring out my art style

At this point, I was still not planning my animations ahead, but I was spending a considerably longer time actually drawing a unique person, instead of just a stick figure. I remember taking photos of my hand in specific positions since it is hard to draw a hand that makes sense without reference, which is still the case for me, althout I have gotten used to the common hand gestures. (I swear I'm not AI TwT)

I made this one at the wise age of 12, I was so wise in fact, I called it "when you phone disturb you" (totally not because I didn't know how to spell). I hadn't named the charectar, but I used him everywhere. Using color only in some places was a design choice that I made back then, that I was super proud of for some reason.

This is a great example for the fact that I didn't plan anything, this started as a joke on what it feels like to go in public, but somehow ended up becoming "Tiktok pranks be like". Don't ask me where the joke is there, I don't even know.
