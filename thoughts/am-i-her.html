Timeline of me Questioning - Am I Her?

23 mar 2026

People think you always find out that you were trans at a very young age, or that trans people were very obviously trans from the beginning. But personally, while there were in fact signs, I had overlooked them all. Either because I didn't have a clear idea on the meaning of transgender, or because of the fact that I just thought it was normal for a cisgender person.

One day, it all just made sense. How though? well, I didn't remember much detail either, but I went through my search history, files, diary entries and some old Reddit posts, to figure out what exactly made my egg crack.

Interesting past eggs

Even before the times I knew what being transgender even was, I remember thinking about how nice it would be to be a girl. But unlike nowadays, those thoughts didn't linger. They were usually just single time things with a few exceptions. I have listed some of the most impactful eggs of mine, that I don't want to, and won't, forget.

  • Way back in pre-school, there was this cosplay thing where you'd reenact something. I don't remember it in detail, but what I do remember is the fact that I had to cosplay as a balloon man. Ugh, cosplaying as a crusty old man with a sharpie-drawn beard. I remember trying to convince my mom to somehow change my role. To what you may ask? of course the role of being a housewife...
  • It was like a solid 2 weeks straight, when I really wanted to be a girl. So much so in fact, I wished to wake up as a girl every night for that period. I have no idea how i did not pick that up as a sign, but I learned lucid dreaming just so I can try living as a girl. At that point, I didn't know i could just... be one so I forgot about that idea eventually after I failed to look at myself in lucid dreams.

  • I was drawing a pixel art drawing of a girl for a game I was working on, and I realised how nice it would be to be a girl like her. Living in a peaceful grassland, guiding a lost player once in a while, ah that would be awesome. I'm sad that I stopped the development of that game, but hopefully I might pick it back up!

  • When I heard the news that in 2017, boys of the Isca Acedamy in the UK wore skirts to school, as they weren't allowed to wear shorts in the hot weather, I wished we could do that too. Not just because of the fact that the weather here is even hotter and we have to wear long trousers, but because I just liked the idea of wearing a skirt. It hit me so hard that I even made a comic about it.

    • Something subconscious

    I can't put into words what it is, but I had something inside that knew something was off. I don't remember most of those cases in exact detail to write here, but I do remember this one day, just a few days before the egg finally cracked and this feeling was at its highest, my mom was pointing out at some girls saying how pretty their dresses were, and that gave me so much gender envy.

    Dec 2025 - Maybe

    I decided to read the book It's perfectly Normal out of pure curiosity on the 25th of December, and I learned a lot from it. Even at this point, I kinda knew something was off, but I never really accepted it because I wasn't sure. One thing led to another, and I was reading The Gender Identity Workbook for Kids by the 26th. I finally learned a bit about what transgender really was. That made me change my Discord pronouns go from a proud he/him to absolutely nothing.

    2025 Christmas and the New year 2026 was miserable, suffering from great gender dysphoria not knowing what I really was. I was feeling sadness, guilt, kind of happy, no appetite and I couldn't even sleep well. This is what I had to say on a reddit post from 30th December...

    Am I really trans or just a feminine boy?

    I thought I was done with this gender dysphoria stuff, but apparently not. First of all, I'm definetly not straight, and I like being called a girl. But at the same time, I feel ashamed. I tried talking with some people on a new discord account, but I feel like a catfish.
    I wish I had a female body, but at the same time, I like my chest flat. Maybe I'm a femboy because I want to wear girly clothes, but I don't want to be called a femBOY.

    Jan 2026 - New year, New me

    I found myself reading The Gender Dysphoria Bible (dw not religious) on the 3rd of January after being recommended in the Reddit comment, and that day marks the end of me being a boy! Okay fine, not so fast. Even though the document was just calling me out with every paragraph, I was still doubting.

    I had joined some transgender communities on Discord, but I wasn't feeling it because I couldn't change my account details without others noticing. Changing the pronouns to they/them felt awesome even though that wasn't exactly what I was aiming for, that was just the next best thing from she/her which was too obvious.

    Feb 2026 - New Discord account, that's me!

    Maybe it was time for a whole new Discord account, even though if it meant forgetting some things. On the 4th of February, I made this account. It IMMEDIATELY felt better. Before I knew it, it was my main account. For once, I could actually represent myself.

    I also made my old Twitter account actually... me, but just don't go on Twitter, it's not worth it.

    Mar 2026 - Where I am now

    Nothing special really happened, I still can't transition due to the situatuion of the world right now, and I'm still doubting sometimes. Generally, things have stabilised, but gender dysphoria still hits hard. the question remains...

    Am I Minnie?
